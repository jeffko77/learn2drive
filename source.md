# Teen Driver Skills Checklist
**Progressive Learning Guide for New Drivers**

## Phase 1: Basic Safety & Vehicle Familiarity
**Master these before the car moves**

### Pre-Drive Safety Checks
- [ ] Adjust seat position (reach pedals comfortably, see over steering wheel)
- [ ] Adjust all mirrors (rearview, both side mirrors)
- [ ] Check seatbelt fits properly across chest and hips
- [ ] Locate and test turn signals, hazard lights, wipers
- [ ] Find parking brake location and operation
- [ ] Identify gas, brake, and clutch pedals (if manual)
- [ ] Check that doors are properly closed and locked

### Basic Vehicle Controls
- [ ] Start and turn off engine safely
- [ ] Understand gear selector (P-R-N-D-L for automatic)
- [ ] Practice smooth acceleration and braking while stationary
- [ ] Use parking brake correctly
- [ ] Operate windshield wipers and lights
- [ ] Adjust climate controls without looking away from road

## Phase 2: Fundamental Driving Skills
**Empty parking lot practice**

### Vehicle Movement Basics
- [ ] Smooth starts from complete stop
- [ ] Gradual, controlled acceleration
- [ ] Progressive braking to smooth stops
- [ ] Straight line driving at consistent speed
- [ ] Gentle steering inputs and corrections
- [ ] Proper following distance judgment (3-second rule)

### Parking Fundamentals
- [ ] Forward parking between lines
- [ ] Reverse parking (backing into space)
- [ ] Parallel parking basics
- [ ] Angle parking (45-degree spaces)
- [ ] Safe distance from other vehicles

## Phase 3: Traffic Navigation
**Quiet residential streets**

### Intersection Skills
- [ ] Complete stops at stop signs (behind the line)
- [ ] Yield right-of-way correctly
- [ ] Traffic light response (green, yellow, red)
- [ ] Safe gap judgment for turns
- [ ] Left turns with oncoming traffic
- [ ] Right turns on red (where legal)

### Lane Management
- [ ] Stay centered in lane
- [ ] Check mirrors every 5-8 seconds
- [ ] Signal 100 feet before turns in city, 300 feet on highway
- [ ] Shoulder checks before changing lanes
- [ ] Merge smoothly with traffic flow

## Phase 4: Intermediate Traffic Skills
**Busier streets and multi-lane roads**

### Speed Management
- [ ] Match traffic flow safely
- [ ] Adjust speed for conditions (weather, visibility)
- [ ] Use cruise control on appropriate roads
- [ ] Recognize and respond to speed limit changes
- [ ] Safe speeds for curves and hills

### Advanced Intersection Navigation
- [ ] Multi-lane intersections
- [ ] Protected vs unprotected left turns
- [ ] Roundabout entry and exit
- [ ] Complex traffic light patterns
- [ ] Pedestrian and cyclist awareness at intersections

## Phase 5: Highway and Freeway Driving
**High-speed, multi-lane traffic**

### Highway Entry and Exit
- [ ] Acceleration lane merging
- [ ] Match traffic speed before merging
- [ ] Safe following distances at highway speeds
- [ ] Exit ramp preparation and execution
- [ ] Lane positioning for upcoming exits

### Highway Maintenance
- [ ] Consistent speed control
- [ ] Passing slower vehicles safely
- [ ] Being passed by faster vehicles
- [ ] Construction zone navigation
- [ ] Rest stop and service area access

## Phase 6: Challenging Conditions
**Weather and visibility challenges**

### Weather Driving
- [ ] Rain driving (reduced speed, increased following distance)
- [ ] Fog navigation (low beam lights, slower speeds)
- [ ] Snow and ice basics (where applicable)
- [ ] Hot weather considerations (tire pressure, overheating)
- [ ] Wind compensation techniques

### Low Visibility Conditions
- [ ] Night driving with proper light use
- [ ] Dawn and dusk adaptation
- [ ] Driving into sun glare
- [ ] Heavy rain visibility techniques
- [ ] Proper use of high and low beam headlights

## Phase 7: Advanced Safety and Emergency Skills
**Defensive driving mastery**

### Hazard Recognition
- [ ] Scan for potential dangers constantly
- [ ] Identify aggressive or impaired drivers
- [ ] Recognize road surface hazards
- [ ] Anticipate pedestrian and cyclist movements
- [ ] Spot mechanical problems in other vehicles

### Emergency Responses
- [ ] Tire blowout recovery
- [ ] Brake failure response
- [ ] Steering failure compensation
- [ ] Skid recovery techniques
- [ ] Safe pullover for emergencies

### Vehicle Breakdown Procedures
- [ ] Safe roadside stopping
- [ ] Hazard light activation
- [ ] Emergency triangle or flare placement
- [ ] When and how to call for help
- [ ] Basic tire changing knowledge

## Phase 8: Independent Driving Readiness
**Real-world navigation and judgment**

### Route Planning and Navigation
- [ ] GPS and map reading while driving safely
- [ ] Alternative route planning
- [ ] Unfamiliar area navigation
- [ ] Parking in crowded areas (malls, events)
- [ ] Time management for trips

### Passenger and Distraction Management
- [ ] Maintaining focus with friends in car
- [ ] Cell phone discipline (hands-free only)
- [ ] Music and entertainment system control
- [ ] Eating/drinking while driving safely
- [ ] Managing multiple passengers

### Advanced Judgment Calls
- [ ] When NOT to drive (fatigue, illness, stress)
- [ ] Recognizing personal skill limitations
- [ ] Refusing rides from impaired drivers
- [ ] Highway vs local route decisions
- [ ] Weather-related driving decisions

---

## Mastery Indicators
**Your teen is ready for independent driving when they can:**

- [ ] Complete all skills without prompting or reminders
- [ ] Demonstrate consistent good judgment across all conditions
- [ ] Handle unexpected situations calmly and safely
- [ ] Show respect for other drivers, pedestrians, and cyclists
- [ ] Take responsibility for vehicle maintenance and safety checks
- [ ] Communicate clearly about driving challenges or concerns

---

## Notes for Parents/Instructors
- Practice each phase thoroughly before moving to the next
- Some skills may need revisiting as complexity increases
- Every teen learns at a different pace - don't rush
- Consider professional instruction for highway and advanced skills
- Document progress and celebrate milestones achieved
- Always model the driving behavior you want to see
